Working Papers

Sectoral Aggregation and Misallocation from Variable Markups

  • Award: University of Michigan Outstanding Third Year Paper
  • [Latest Version]

This paper studies how sectoral aggregation affects misallocation estimates in models with variable markups. In a nested CES framework with oligopolistic competition, misallocation depends on markup dispersion within and across sectors, weighted by substitution elasticities. Applying this to European firm-level data reveals two countervailing biases. First, higher aggregation (for example, grouping meat and bakery into a broader food sector) mechanically smooths out dispersion, reducing measured misallocation. Second, aggregation biases down estimated elasticities. The mechanical bias typically dominates: using aggregated sectoral definitions leads to a quantitatively significant underestimation of aggregate productivity losses.
